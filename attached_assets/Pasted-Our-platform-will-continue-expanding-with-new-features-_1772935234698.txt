Our platform will continue expanding with new features designed to improve usability, engagement, and financial learning for both students and teachers. Within the simulator, users will be able to smoothly scroll back and forth through historical market performance to better understand trends and investment timing. Each lesson will include interactive quizzes at the end to reinforce learning, while lessons themselves will be expanded with wider categories, improved sorting, and better organization so students can easily navigate topics such as investing strategies, diversification, and risk management. The dashboard and watchlist will include an improved investment interface with a scroll wheel when selecting investment amounts, allowing users to quickly adjust their positions. When a new user first signs up, they will be guided through a short animated tutorial that explains how the simulator, watchlist, lessons, and portfolio management tools work so they can start investing immediately. Leaderboards will also become more dynamic, allowing users to switch between global rankings and local rankings within their school or classroom. To increase motivation and progression, account levels will be introduced so students unlock new features, achievements, and recognition as they learn and participate more within the platform. 

To make the experience visually engaging and fun, each stock on the watchlist will include a company logo or image so students can quickly recognize companies they are following. The Tips section will also be expanded to include more market insights, helping students understand why markets move and how investors make decisions. Users will be able to set price notifications on both their simulator investments and watchlist assets so they receive alerts when a stock reaches a certain value, with these alerts appearing inside a dedicated notifications system. A new Risk Calculator will be added where the previous terminal feature existed, allowing students to analyze how risky their portfolio is and understand the concept of diversification and exposure. The login system will also be improved so users can sign in using either their username or email address, making access simpler and more flexible. 

A major expansion will focus on the classroom and school system. Teachers will be able to create assignments that students must complete, such as building a diversified portfolio, analyzing a company, or achieving a specific return. Students will have their own classroom dashboard where they can view assignments, track their progress, see their class leaderboard, and interact with their teacher’s learning objectives. When schools choose the school plan, users will be required to select their school during setup so classes and rankings remain organized. The admin system will also include a financial management section so administrators can manage school subscriptions, plans, and billing information. 

The school system itself will become a major separate experience within the platform. When a school selects the education plan, they will enter a new animated onboarding area introducing the platform with visual transitions and messages such as “Welcome to 12Digits – finance made easy for your students.” This section will showcase the educational tools, interactive simulations, classroom competitions, and learning features through smooth animations and visually engaging demonstrations. As teachers scroll through this area, different features will appear dynamically, highlighting portfolio simulations, classroom leaderboards, lesson systems, quizzes, and financial education tools. Once teachers reach the end of this section, they will see a clear call to action reading “Satisfied with us? Begin here today,” accompanied by animated elements guiding them to set up their school account. At this stage, teachers will be able to enter their details such as name, school, and role, generate a custom quote for their institution, and complete the onboarding process. Access to this system will be restricted so only verified teachers can create and manage school environments. 

Within the school environment, the platform will provide a full financial learning experience where students participate in a simulated economy while also learning investing skills. Students will earn income through classroom activities, manage virtual bank balances, invest in simulated markets, and make decisions about saving, spending, and long-term investing. Teachers will be able to control the classroom economy and assign financial challenges that simulate real-world situations. Unlike traditional classroom tools, the platform will also include interactive games and challenges related to investing, risk management, and financial planning, helping students learn through exploration and competition rather than just theory. By combining interactive lessons, gamified learning, investment simulations, and classroom management tools, the system will provide a highly engaging environment where students develop real financial knowledge while teachers maintain full oversight of classroom progress and performance.
