The existing school system should remain the foundation of the platform, but it now needs to be expanded into a fully integrated financial education environment that combines investing education with a classroom economy. Teachers will continue using the current School World system that was previously created, and this should remain consistent with the existing structure and design, but new mechanics should be layered into it. The core idea is that students are first taught investing concepts by their teacher, including topics such as stocks, diversification, portfolio management, risk, and market behavior, and they will practice these lessons through the platform’s stock market simulator and learning modules. As students complete lessons, quizzes, assignments, and investment activities, they earn classroom currency or virtual money as rewards for their learning and performance. This earned money becomes part of the classroom economy and allows students to interact with additional financial systems that simulate real-world money management. Students can use their earnings to pay simulated expenses such as rent, utilities, and taxes, participate in classroom auctions where they can bid on rewards or privileges, save their money to gain interest, or participate in classroom challenges and competitions. Teachers must have full administrative control to assign rewards, create or modify expenses, trigger financial events, run auctions, assign classroom jobs, and monitor student financial progress. The platform should also support optional classroom jobs where students earn small consistent income, but the main focus should remain on earning money through learning and investing activities so that financial education drives the system rather than the economy alone. In addition to this, the system should support classroom businesses, collaborative investment challenges, mini financial games, and random economic events that simulate real-world situations such as unexpected expenses or rewards. All of these systems must integrate with the current School World structure that already exists so the experience remains consistent and seamless. It is also very important that students only have access to what we call “School World,” meaning when a student logs in using their school account or join code, they should only see the School World interface and features rather than the normal platform layout used for regular accounts. Their dashboard, simulator, lessons, and classroom systems should all appear inside the School World design and visual style so the environment feels like its own dedicated educational world. Teachers, however, should retain additional administrative tools and controls within the School World system that students cannot access. The final goal is to maintain the current school system that already exists while expanding it into a richer and more interactive financial learning environment that combines investing education, classroom economies, games, competitions, and teacher-controlled financial simulations, all within the School World interface.
